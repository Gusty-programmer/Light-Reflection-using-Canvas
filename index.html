Light reflection

Light reflection is the phenomenon of changing the direction of light propagation at the surface separating two media, the light returning to the medium from which it came. It appears at the interface between two optical media.

The laws of reflection

    There are two laws:
  • The incident light ray, the reflected light ray, and the normal to the surface are coplanar at the point of incidence.
  • The angle of reflection r is equal to the angle of incidence i.

Light refraction

Light refraction Changing the direction of light propagation when passing from a transparent medium to another transparent medium.

The laws of reflection

    There are two laws:
  • The incident light ray, the reflected light ray, and the normal to the surface are coplanar at the point of incidence.
  • The angle of reflection r is not equal to the angle of incidence i.
